# Success Definition

Genesis does not measure success by code volume, agent count, model size, fundraising, or hype.

Success is defined by impact.

Genesis succeeds when:

- Users accomplish more than they otherwise could.
- Small teams outperform expectations.
- Founders launch businesses that would never have existed otherwise.
- Knowledge becomes durable rather than fragile.
- Organizational capabilities become widely accessible.
- Human effort shifts from repetitive work toward creative work.
- The platform becomes increasingly self-improving without becoming less accountable.

A future where one determined individual can effectively operate with the leverage of a much larger organization is the north star against which all success is measured.

Anything that advances that outcome is progress.

Anything that undermines that outcome should be challenged regardless of short-term benefit.
