# Anti-Patterns

## Purpose

This document exists to describe outcomes Genesis must intentionally avoid.

History shows that organizations often fail not because they lack goals but because they fail to identify unacceptable behaviors early.

These anti-patterns serve as warning signs for future contributors and agents.

---

## Black Box Intelligence

Genesis must never require blind trust.

Users should be able to understand:

- what occurred
- why it occurred
- what produced it
- what evidence supports it

Opaque systems are fragile systems.

---

## Vendor Lock-In

Genesis should avoid creating dependency on:

- specific cloud providers
- specific model providers
- specific databases
- specific hosting vendors
- proprietary formats

Portability is a design requirement.

---

## Knowledge Silos

No critical knowledge should exist exclusively:

- inside a person's memory
- inside a conversation
- inside an undocumented decision
- inside an isolated agent

Knowledge must be externalized.

---

## Hero Culture

Genesis must never depend upon a single individual.

The Founder is responsible for initiating the vision.

The system must ultimately become capable of surviving beyond the Founder.

---

## Feature Hoarding

Features are not success.

Capability is success.

Adding features without improving capability creates complexity while generating little value.

---

## Invisible Technical Debt

Debt may be accepted.

Debt may not be hidden.

Any intentional tradeoff should be documented.

---

## Governance Theater

Rules that are not enforced create false confidence.

Governance exists to shape behavior, not decorate documentation.

---

## Unbounded Autonomy

Autonomy without accountability creates risk.

Authority must always remain proportional to responsibility.

---

## Organizational Amnesia

The loss of knowledge is one of the greatest threats to project continuity.

Systems that repeatedly relearn the same lessons are failing to preserve intelligence.

Genesis must actively resist organizational forgetting.
