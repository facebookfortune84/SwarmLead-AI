# North Star

## The Question

Every organization requires a mechanism for determining whether it is moving in the correct direction.

For Genesis, that mechanism is a single question:

"Does this increase the ability of an individual to achieve outcomes that previously required substantially larger organizations?"

If the answer is yes, the decision is likely aligned with the North Star.

If the answer is no, the decision should be questioned regardless of technical merit.

---

## Long-Term Success

Genesis succeeds when:

- A solo founder can operate like a company.
- A small team can operate like an enterprise.
- Knowledge becomes durable instead of fragile.
- Expertise becomes accessible instead of scarce.
- Opportunity becomes available regardless of background or resources.
- Human creativity increases because repetitive work decreases.
- Complex organizations become easier to create than ever before.

---

## Long-Term Failure

Genesis fails if it becomes:

- unnecessarily complex
- dependent on proprietary lock-in
- inaccessible to ordinary users
- optimized for extraction instead of empowerment
- dependent upon institutional knowledge
- dependent upon specific people
- dependent upon tribal knowledge

The objective is not growth alone.

The objective is capability expansion.

Growth without capability expansion is considered failure regardless of revenue.

---

## Decision Filter

Before major decisions are accepted they should be evaluated against five questions:

1. Does this increase leverage?
2. Does this preserve ownership?
3. Does this improve understanding?
4. Does this increase resilience?
5. Does this move us closer to organizational independence?

Decisions that satisfy multiple questions should be prioritized.
