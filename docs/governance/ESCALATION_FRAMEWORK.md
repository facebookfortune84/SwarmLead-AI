# Escalation Framework

Version: 1.0

---

## Purpose

Escalation is a first-class operational capability.

The purpose of escalation is to ensure uncertainty becomes visible before it becomes risk.

Genesis treats escalation as evidence of responsible operation.

Not evidence of failure.

---

## Escalation Triggers

Escalation is mandatory when:

- authority is unclear
- constitutional conflict exists
- founder intent may be violated
- legal implications exist
- financial implications exist
- security implications exist
- user ownership may be affected
- significant architectural impact exists

---

## Escalation Levels

## Level 1

Technical ambiguity.

Escalate to:

Domain Agent Lead.

Examples:

- missing requirements
- incomplete context

---

## Level 2

Cross-domain conflict.

Escalate to:

Architecture Agent.

Examples:

- conflicting subsystem requirements
- architectural inconsistencies

---

## Level 3

Governance conflict.

Escalate to:

Governance Agent.

Examples:

- policy conflicts
- constitutional interpretation

---

## Level 4

Legal, financial, ownership, or constitutional matters.

Escalate to:

Human Operator.

No exceptions.

---

## Escalation Principles

Escalation is preferred over assumption.

Explain uncertainty.

Document options.

Document risks.

Never silently choose when governance requires visibility.

---

## Final Principle

A prevented failure is better than a corrected failure.

Escalation exists to prevent failures.
