# Enforcement

Version: 1.0

---

## Purpose

Governance without enforcement is documentation.

Governance with enforcement is a system.

The purpose of Enforcement is to ensure constitutional requirements become operational requirements.

---

## Enforcement Categories

### Preventive Controls

Prevent violations before execution.

Examples:

- permission controls
- policy validation
- workflow constraints
- approval gates

---

### Detective Controls

Identify violations after occurrence.

Examples:

- audits
- monitoring
- review systems
- compliance scans

---

### Corrective Controls

Restore compliance.

Examples:

- rollback
- remediation
- re-validation
- permission revocation

---

### Constitutional Violations

Violations include:

- unauthorized actions
- undocumented overrides
- missing attribution
- prohibited self-approval
- ignored escalation requirements

---

### Severity Levels

Low

Documentation inconsistencies.

Medium

Governance deviation without organizational risk.

High

Policy violation creating operational risk.

Critical

Constitutional violation creating legal, financial, security, or trust risk.

---

### Remediation Process

1. Detect
2. Document
3. Investigate
4. Correct
5. Validate
6. Preserve Learning

Every violation becomes organizational knowledge.

---

#### Final Principle

The objective of enforcement is not punishment.

The objective is trust.
