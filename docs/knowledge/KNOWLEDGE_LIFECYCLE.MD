# Knowledge Lifecycle

Version: 1.0

---

## Purpose

Knowledge is an asset.

Like all assets, it has a lifecycle.

This document defines how knowledge enters, evolves within, and persists throughout Genesis.

---

## Stage 1 — Discovery

Knowledge is discovered through:

- Development
- Research
- Testing
- Incidents
- Governance
- Operations

---

## Stage 2 — Codification

Knowledge becomes structured.

Examples:

- Documentation
- ADRs
- Runbooks
- Standards

---

## Stage 3 — Validation

Knowledge is reviewed.

Evidence is gathered.

Assumptions are challenged.

---

## Stage 4 — Integration

Knowledge enters:

- Knowledge Graph
- Repository Intelligence
- RAG

---

## Stage 5 — Retrieval

Knowledge becomes accessible.

Humans and agents can use it.

---

## Stage 6 — Evolution

Knowledge is updated.

Obsolete information is superseded.

History remains preserved.

---

## Stage 7 — Preservation

Knowledge survives:

- Personnel changes
- Architecture changes
- Technology changes
- Organizational changes

---

## Final Principle

Lessons learned once should never need to be learned twice.
